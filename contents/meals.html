The Maldives Launch Voyage

Route

It takes place from Colombo, Sri Lanka to Malé, the capital of the Maldives.

From where to where: The journey is from Colombo Harbour in Sri Lanka across approximately 400 miles of open sea to Malé in the Maldives archipelago.

All Characters In This Passage

  • Gavin Young (the narrator/author)
  • A good bird man (unnamed, who arranged the passage)
  • A bad bird man (Sea Eagle) - described as sitting in an office in Chatham Street, cold, white-haired and hunched over like a vindictive sea eagle, representing the owners of the Maldivian launch
  • The launch captain (Captain Azia Ali) - a thin young man with big teeth
  • Hassan Ali - a crewman seen at the helm, later distressed about confiscated Polaroid pictures
  • Musa - a crewman who spoke good English and had health concerns
  • Ibrahim - a Maldivian crewman who explained the meaning of "Feni, feni, sarna"
  • Ali Qasim - a crewman
  • The Starling Cook - the ship's cook, described as more or less the colour of a starling, very dark in blue shorts and shirt, short and fat-bodied with skinny bowlegs, long bare feet, very little neck, hunched shoulders, long arms, and a beak-like nose
  • The Black Devil (the Starling Cook's assistant) - face very black and knobbly, usually grinning
  • A young Australian passenger - taciturn, shy, travelling slowly to Europe, asking "What is Time?"
  • Two Maldivian passengers who travelled on the cabin roof and offered compressed fish (Maldive fish/biltong)
  • Sam Kadirgamar, QC - who suggested the Maldives visit
  • Tom Abraham - Indian high commissioner in Colombo who helped with Andamans permit
  • Mr Missier - shipping agent in Colombo
  • General manager of Ceylon Shipping Lines
  • Mr Missier's future son-in-law
  • Various unnamed crew members (six men plus the captain)

Detailed Examination Focusing On Food, Ingredients, Drinks, And Preparations

Food and drink mentions in the Maldives launch voyage passage:

1. Coconut Preparation

  • "I now know how to open a coconut with a spanner" - indicates coconuts were available and being prepared
  • Detailed description: "I saw, for example, how he opened a coconut with a spanner, holding it in one hand, the spanner in the other, and tapping it with many small, firm strokes around its middle until it fell apart in two neat halves. He scraped each half against the upturned sharp metal prong of what looked like a shoehorn, which he clamped between his knees; then he wrapped the gratings in a porous cloth, and dunked them into a wide pan of water and rice."

2. Starling Cook's Preparations

  • Stove: "a small metal stick-burning stove about a foot high" near the bows
  • Breakfast: "made tea and nan - flat, oily bread - with a very sweet form of custard"
  • Pineapple service: "cut and served pineapple chunks at night"
  • Betel preparation: offering "pan, the tiny sandwich of betel leaf, areca nut and lime"
  • First day dinner: "rice, dried fish, diced and curried potatoes, onions and raw chillies"
  • After storm: "shaping lumps of dough into white cricket balls, and then rolling them out flat on a hot plate on the stove" with "jam and butter to make a very sweet custard" and "dark, sweet tea"
  • Final evening: "cooked a magnificent curry full of chilli and pungent exotic leaves"

3. Black Devil's Preparations

  • Made tea for Maldivian passengers
  • Played tricks on passengers (unspecified)
  • "quietly continued to grind a stick of cinnamon to powder with a stone rolling-pin"

4. Specific Food Items Mentioned

  • Pineapples (mentioned as cargo and served as chunks)
  • Rice (cooked in seawater)
  • Dried fish
  • Potatoes (diced and curried)
  • Onions
  • Raw chillies
  • Nan (flat, oily bread)
  • Sweet custard (made from jam and butter)
  • Tea (served multiple times: breakfast, after storm, and frequently by Black Devil)
  • Curry (magnificent curry with chilli and pungent exotic leaves)
  • Betel leaf preparations (pan with areca nut and lime)
  • Compressed fish (Maldive fish/biltong) offered by Maldivian passengers
  • Cinnamon (ground to powder by Black Devil)

5. Drinks Mentioned

  • Tea (served frequently: sweet tea, dark sweet tea)
  • Seawater (used for cooking rice and cleaning pots/pans)
  • Fresh water (from tanks, used for drinking and washing)
  • Beer (mentioned in conversation with Musa: "You drink beer?" "Yes, I drink beer. Muslims also drink beer.")
  • Barley water (mentioned earlier when Mr Missier offered it to Gavin in Colombo)

6. Food Preparation Details

  • The launch had a functional stick-burning stove
  • Seawater was used for cooking and cleaning (no fresh water wasted on non-drinking uses)
  • The Starling Cook had specific implements: "a huge soot-black kettle and two deep cooking pans" plus "a wide, open tray" for sorting rice
  • Provisions stored included: "little sacks of cabbages, beans, leaves, chillies (green and red), cardamom, small cumin seeds, coriander, black peppers, cinnamon and garlic"
  • The chimney acted as a stove: "The chimney seems to be red-hot, and acts like a stove in the already overheated, confined space of the cabin"
  • Cargo included pineapples, bamboo furniture, ceramic lavatory bowls, and compressed wood panels

7. Eating Conditions

  • Meals eaten on diesel barrels or coils of rope
  • Eaten in silence with fingers
  • No tea served with meals, only tepid fresh water from the tank
  • After storm: tea and sweet custard with bread
  • Final celebration: magnificent curry with singing and dancing

Observations

The passage provides rich detail about the challenging conditions of the voyage ("We are nearly on our beam ends once every minute and a half") contrasted with the nurturing food preparations by the Starling Cook and his assistant, highlighting how food and drink served as both sustenance and comfort during the difficult journey.
